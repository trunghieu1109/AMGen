We have a tetrahedron with opposite edges equal: AB=CD=√41, AC=BD=√80, AD=BC=√89. Such an "isosceles" tetrahedron has all four faces congruent, each a triangle with side‐squares 41,80,89. 

1. Compute face area T: for sides a^2=41,b^2=80,c^2=89,
   16T^2 = 2(ab)^2+2(bc)^2+2(ca)^2 – (a^4+b^4+c^4)
           =2(41·80+80·89+89·41) – (41^2+80^2+89^2)
           =2·14049 – 16002 =28098–16002=12096,
   so T^2=756, T=6√21.  Total surface S=4T=24√21.
