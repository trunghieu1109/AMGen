We seek two‐digit base-b numbers n=ab+c (with 1≤a≤b−1,0≤c≤b−1) satisfying a+c=√n, i.e. (a+c)²=ab+c.  Set s=a+c so ab+c=s² ⇒ c=s²−ab and also c=s−a ⇒ s−a=s²−ab ⇒ s²−s=a(b−1), so a=s(s−1)/(b−1).  Hence b−1 must divide s(s−1).  Writing d=b−1, s runs 1…d, and s(s−1)≡0 mod d iff for each prime p|d, s≡0 or 1 mod p^{e}. To exceed ten beautiful numbers need 2^k−1>10 ⇒2^k≥12⇒k≥4.  The smallest d with 4 distinct primes is 2·3·5·7=210, so b=d+1=211, yielding 2^4−1=15 solutions.  No smaller b can give >10.
