We are given a 13th root of unity \( \omega \neq 1 \), and we want to find the remainder when
\[
\prod_{k=0}^{12} (2 - 2\omega^k + \omega^{2k})

Step 1: Understand the roots of unity
Since \(\omega\) is a primitive 13th root of unity, \(\omega^{13} = 1\) and \(\omega^k \neq 1\) for \(0 < k < 13\).

Step 2: Simplify the expression inside the product
Consider the term inside the product:
\[
2 - 2\omega^k + \omega^{2k}.
\]
Rewrite it as:
\[
2 - 2\omega^k + (\omega^k)^2 = (\omega^k)^2 - 2\omega^k + 2.
\]
This looks like a quadratic in \(\omega^k\):
\[
x^2 - 2x + 2 \quad \text{where} \quad x = \omega^k.
\]

Step 3: Factor or find roots of the quadratic
The quadratic equation \(x^2 - 2x + 2 = 0\) has roots:
\[
x = \frac{2 \pm \sqrt{4 - 8}}{2} = 1 \pm i.
\]

Step 4: Express the product in terms of \(x = \omega^k\)
So each term is \((\omega^k)^2 - 2\omega^k + 2 = (\omega^k - (1+i))(\omega^k - (1 - i))\).

\[
\prod_{k=0}^{12} (2 - 2\omega^k + \omega^{2k}) = \prod_{k=0}^{12} (\omega^k - (1+i)) \cdot \prod_{k=0}^{12} (\omega^k - (1 - i)).
\]

The roots of \(x^{13} - 1 = 0\) are exactly \(\omega^k, k=0,\dots,12\).

\[
\prod_{k=0}^{12} (x - \omega^k) = x^{13} - 1.
