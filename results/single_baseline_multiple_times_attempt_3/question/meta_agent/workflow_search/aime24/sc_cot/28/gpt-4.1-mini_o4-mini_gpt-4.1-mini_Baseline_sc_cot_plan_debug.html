We orient the torus so its axis is vertical, translate it along the vertical so it is tangent to the sphere along a horizontal circle of radius s.  Writing cylindrical coordinates (r,z), the torus is ((r–6)^2+(z–h)^2=9) and the sphere is (r^2+z^2=121).  Tangency requires these two curves meet and also have parallel normals, which gives an extra equation (z–h)/z=(r–6)/r.
