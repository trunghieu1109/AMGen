We have the family of unit‐length segments with x‐intercept p and y‐intercept q satisfying p^2+q^2=1, hence the line equations x/p+y/q=1. Their envelope satisfies simultaneously x/p+y/q=1 and its derivative wrt p zero. Eliminating gives the envelope curve x=p^3, y=(1−p^2)^{3/2}. The desired point C is the unique intersection of this envelope with line AB (the special segment of the family), whose equation is 2x+2y/√3=1.
