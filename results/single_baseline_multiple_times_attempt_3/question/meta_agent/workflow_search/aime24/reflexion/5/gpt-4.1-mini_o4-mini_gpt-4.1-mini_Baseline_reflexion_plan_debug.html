We recognize that the tetrahedron has opposite edges equal, so all four faces are congruent triangles with sides sqrt(41), sqrt(80), sqrt(89). We compute the volume via the Cayley–Menger determinant: det = 819200, so V^2 = 819200/288 = 25600/9, hence V = 160/3. Each triangular face has area by Heron’s formula: (1/4)√[(a+b+c)(-a+b+c)(a-b+c)(a+b-c)] with a^2=41, b^2=80, c^2=89 gives area = 6√21. Thus total surface area S = 24√21.
