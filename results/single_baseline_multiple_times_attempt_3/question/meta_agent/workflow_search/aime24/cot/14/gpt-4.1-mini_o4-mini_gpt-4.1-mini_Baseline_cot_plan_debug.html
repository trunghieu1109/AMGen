We seek rhombi ABCD inscribed in the hyperbola x^2/20 – y^2/24 = 1 with diagonals crossing at O.  Let A=(x1,y1), C=(–x1,–y1), B=(x2,y2), D=(–x2,–y2).  Being a parallelogram with perpendicular diagonals (a rhombus) gives x1x2 + y1y2 = 0.  Both A and B lie on the hyperbola:
  x1^2/20 – y1^2/24 = 1,  x2^2/20 – y2^2/24 = 1.
Parametrize (x1,y1) ⟂ (x2,y2) by (x1,y1)=k(–y2,x2).  Substituting into A’s hyperbola equation yields
