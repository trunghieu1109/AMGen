$\bullet$ The sum of the items in the list is $30$.
$\bullet$ The unique mode of the list is $9$.
$\bullet$ The median of the list is a positive integer that does not appear in the list itself.

assistant 
Let the list have 2k entries (even, since the median is not itself in the list).  The unique mode is 9 so 9 must appear more times than any other value; the smallest possibility is that 9 appears twice and every other element appears exactly once.  Then the sum of the two 9’s is 18, so the remaining entries must sum to 12 and be distinct positives ≠9.  With 2k=4 (so k=2), there are two other entries summing to 12; the only choice that gives an integer median not in the list is {5,7}, yielding the sorted list [5,7,9,9], whose median is (7+9)/2=8 (not in the list) and whose sum is 30.  Sum of squares is 5²+7²+9²+9²=25+49+81+81=236.
