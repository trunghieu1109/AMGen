Since the diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other and intersect at the origin, the diagonals are vectors \(\vec{BD} = 2\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{AC} = 2\vec{u}\) for some vectors \(\vec{u}\) and \(\vec{v}\) originating from the origin. The vertices are then \( \pm \vec{u} \) and \( \pm \vec{v} \). The rhombus condition means all sides are equal in length, so \(|\vec{u} - \vec{v}| = |\vec{u} + \vec{v}| \). This implies \( \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 0 \), so \(\vec{u}\) and \(\vec{v}\) are perpendicular vectors.

Since \(A, B, C, D\) lie on the hyperbola, \(\vec{u} = (x_1, y_1)\), \(\vec{v} = (x_2, y_2)\) satisfy the hyperbola equation:

\[
\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 0 \implies \sqrt{20} \cosh t \cdot \sqrt{20} \cosh s + \sqrt{24} \sinh t \cdot \sqrt{24} \sinh s = 0
