We place the torus (major radius 6, minor radius 3) with its axis vertical and center of its generating circle at height h above the sphere’s center.  A point on the torus is
P=(6+3cosφ)e_r+(h+3sinφ)e_z.
At tangency to the sphere of radius 11 we require the torus normal (direction from its generating‐circle center to P) to align with the sphere normal (direction OP).  This leads to (6+3cosφ)/11=s cosφ and (h+3sinφ)/11=s sinφ with s=±1.
