We seek maximal placements of white/black chips on 5×5 grid so each row/column (if nonempty) is monochromatic and no empty cell can accept any chip without breaking monotonicity.  One shows that in any maximal configuration every row and every column must already be assigned a color (white or black), and then a cell is filled iff its row and column colors agree.  Conversely each of the 2^5 assignments of colors to rows and the 2^5 assignments to columns yields a unique maximal configuration.  Hence the total number is 2^5·2^5=2^10=1024.
