The sphere S has radius 11. When the torus is externally tangent to the sphere, the major radius of the torus plus the sphere's radius equals the distance from the torus' center to the tangent point. This gives us two scenarios: when the torus is resting inside the sphere and when it is resting outside the sphere.

1. When T is inside S and externally tangent: The distance from the center of the sphere to the center of the torus is 11 - 3 = 8. The radius of the tangent circle r_i is the distance from the center of the torus to the tangent point, which is 6 - 8 = -2, but since it is a radius, take the positive value: r_i = 2.

The difference r_i - r_o = 2 - 8 = -6.
