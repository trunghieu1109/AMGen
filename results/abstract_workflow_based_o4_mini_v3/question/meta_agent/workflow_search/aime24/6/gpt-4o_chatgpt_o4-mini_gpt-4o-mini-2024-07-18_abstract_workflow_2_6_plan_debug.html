We seek the smallest radius r of a sphere that can contain any rectangular box with surface area 54 and volume 23. Such a box has edges l,w,h satisfying
  lw+lh+wh = 27   (since 2(lw+lh+wh)=54)
  lwh = 23.
Any containing sphere must have radius at least half the space diagonal d = √(l²+w²+h²), so r² = (l²+w²+h²)/4.  To cover all boxes in B we choose the box maximizing L = l²+w²+h² under the two constraints.  By symmetry one finds the critical cases occur when two edges are equal, say l=w=a.  Then:
  a²+2a h = 27,
  a² h = 23.
