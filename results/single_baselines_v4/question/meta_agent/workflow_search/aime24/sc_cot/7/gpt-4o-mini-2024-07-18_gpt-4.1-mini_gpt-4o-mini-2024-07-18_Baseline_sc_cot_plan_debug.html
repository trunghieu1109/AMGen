\[
 y^x = x^{10}.

From \( \log_y(x^{4y}) = 10 \), we have:
\[
 x^{4y} = y^{10}.

Step 2: Take natural logarithms (ln) on both sides of each equation.

\[

\[

\]

\[
 x b = 10 a \\ 4 y a = 10 b.
\]

So the equations become:
\[
 e^a b = 10 a \\ 4 e^b a = 10 b.
\]

\[
 b = \frac{10 a}{e^a}.
\]

\[
 4 e^b a = 10 b = 10 \cdot \frac{10 a}{e^a} = \frac{100 a}{e^a}.

\[

\[
 e^{a+b} = e^{\ln x + \ln y} = e^{\ln(xy)} = xy = 25.
\]
