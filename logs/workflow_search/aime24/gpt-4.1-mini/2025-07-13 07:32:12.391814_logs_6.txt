1. Extract and Summarize Given Information:
- We have a set \( \mathcal{B} \) of rectangular boxes (rectangular parallelepipeds) with surface area \( S = 54 \) and volume \( V = 23 \).
- Each box is defined by three positive edge lengths \( x, y, z \).
- Surface area formula: \( 2(xy + yz + zx) = 54 \).
- Volume formula: \( xyz = 23 \).
- The problem defines \( r \) as the radius of the smallest sphere that can contain each box in \( \mathcal{B} \).
- The goal is to find \( r^2 = \frac{p}{q} \) in lowest terms and compute \( p + q \).

2. Analyze Relationships Between Components:
- The boxes vary but must satisfy the two constraints on surface area and volume.
- The smallest sphere containing a box must at least contain its space diagonal, since the sphere must cover the entire box.
- The radius \( r \) of the smallest sphere containing a box with edges \( x, y, z \) is at least half the space diagonal length \( d = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2} \), so \( r = \frac{d}{2} \).
- Since \( r \) must contain every box in \( \mathcal{B} \), \( r \) is the maximum over all boxes of half the space diagonal.
- The problem reduces to maximizing \( x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \) subject to \( 2(xy + yz + zx) = 54 \) and \( xyz = 23 \).
- The constraints link the variables nonlinearly, and the problem is an optimization under nonlinear constraints.
