1. Extract and Summarize Given Information:
- Aya walks a fixed distance of 9 kilometers every morning.
- She stops at a coffee shop after the walk, spending t minutes there.
- When walking at speed s km/h, the total time (walk + coffee break) is 4 hours.
- When walking at speed s + 2 km/h, the total time (walk + coffee break) is 2 hours 24 minutes (which is 2.4 hours).
- The coffee break time t is the same in both cases.
- The problem asks: If Aya walks at speed s + 0.5 km/h, what is the total time (walk + coffee break) in minutes?

2. Analyze Relationships Between Components:
- The total time consists of walking time plus coffee break time t.
- Walking time = distance / speed = 9 / speed hours.
- Total time at speed s: (9 / s) + (t / 60) = 4 hours.
- Total time at speed s + 2: (9 / (s + 2)) + (t / 60) = 2.4 hours.
- The coffee break time t is constant and independent of walking speed.
- These two equations can be used to solve for s and t.
- Once s and t are known, total time at speed s + 0.5 is (9 / (s + 0.5)) + (t / 60) hours, which can be converted to minutes.

- The problem is primarily algebraic, involving solving equations with variables.
