1. Extract and Summarize Given Information:
- Aya walks a fixed distance of 9 kilometers every morning.
- She stops at a coffee shop after the walk, spending t minutes there.
- When walking at speed s km/h, the total time (walking + coffee break) is 4 hours.
- When walking at speed s + 2 km/h, the total time (walking + coffee break) is 2 hours 24 minutes (which is 2.4 hours).
- The coffee break duration t (in minutes) is the same in both scenarios.
- The problem also considers a third walking speed: s + 0.5 km/h, and asks for the total time including the coffee break.

2. Analyze Relationships Between Components:
- The total time in each scenario is the sum of walking time and coffee break time.
- Walking time = distance / speed = 9 / speed.
- Total time at speed s: 4 hours = (9 / s) + (t / 60).
- Total time at speed s + 2: 2.4 hours = (9 / (s + 2)) + (t / 60).
- The coffee break time t is constant across scenarios, linking the two equations.
- These relationships form a system of equations with two unknowns: s and t.
- The third speed s + 0.5 km/h is used to find a new total time using the same coffee break duration t.

- The problem lies primarily in algebra, involving rational equations.
- It also touches on units conversion (minutes to hours).
- The context is a real-world application of rate, time, and distance relationships.
