1. Extract and Summarize Given Information:
- Aya walks a fixed distance of 9 kilometers every morning.
- She stops at a coffee shop after the walk, spending t minutes there.
- When walking at speed s km/h, the total time (walk + coffee) is 4 hours.
- When walking at speed s + 2 km/h, the total time (walk + coffee) is 2 hours 24 minutes (which is 2.4 hours).
- The coffee shop time t is the same in both cases.
- The problem asks: If Aya walks at speed s + 0.5 km/h, what is the total time (walk + coffee) in minutes?

2. Analyze Relationships Between Components:
- The total time includes walking time plus coffee time t.
- Walking time = distance / speed = 9 / speed hours.
- For speed s: total time = (9 / s) + (t / 60) = 4 hours.
- For speed s + 2: total time = (9 / (s + 2)) + (t / 60) = 2.4 hours.
- The coffee time t is constant and unknown.
- These two equations can be used to solve for s and t.
- Once s and t are known, the total time for speed s + 0.5 can be found as (9 / (s + 0.5)) + (t / 60).

3. Identify the Field of Study:
- This problem lies in the domain of algebra and arithmetic word problems.
- It involves linear equations and rational expressions.
- It is typical of problems found in algebra courses, mathematical competitions, or standardized tests.

4. Highlight Aspects Needing Clarification:
- The problem assumes constant walking speeds and a fixed coffee time t.
- It is implied that the coffee time t is the same regardless of walking speed.
- The units of t are minutes, so conversion to hours is necessary for consistency.
- No ambiguity in the problem statement; all necessary information is provided.
- Potential challenge: solving the system of equations involving s and t.
- Assumptions: walking speed is constant during each walk; coffee time does not depend on walking speed.
