1. Extract and Summarize Given Information:
- Aya walks a fixed distance of 9 kilometers every morning.
- She stops at a coffee shop after the walk, spending t minutes there.
- When walking at speed s km/h, the total time (walking + coffee shop) is 4 hours.
- When walking at speed s + 2 km/h, the total time (walking + coffee shop) is 2 hours 24 minutes (which is 2.4 hours).
- The coffee shop time t (in minutes) is the same in both scenarios.
- The problem also considers walking at speed s + 0.5 km/h and asks for the total time including t.

2. Analyze Relationships Between Components:
- The total time in each scenario is the sum of walking time and coffee shop time t.
- Walking time = distance / speed = 9 / speed.
- The coffee shop time t is constant across scenarios.
- Two equations can be formed:
  4 hours = (9 / s) + (t / 60)
  2.4 hours = (9 / (s + 2)) + (t / 60)
- These equations link s and t, allowing determination of both.
- The problem then asks for total time when speed is s + 0.5 km/h, which would be (9 / (s + 0.5)) + (t / 60).

- The problem lies primarily in algebra and arithmetic.
- It involves solving linear equations with variables representing speed and time.
- Concepts include rates, time-distance relationships, and unit conversions.
- Such problems are common in mathematical competitions and real-life applications involving motion and scheduling.

4. Highlight Aspects Needing Clarification:
- The variable t is given as minutes but appears in equations with hours; conversion is necessary.
- It is assumed that the coffee shop time t is the same in both scenarios, which is critical.
- No explicit constraints on s (e.g., positive speed) are stated but implied.
- The problem assumes constant speeds and no other delays.
- Potential challenge: correctly converting units and solving for two variables from two equations.
