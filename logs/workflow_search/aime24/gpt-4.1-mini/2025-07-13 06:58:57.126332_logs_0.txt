1. Extract and Summarize Given Information:
- Aya walks a fixed distance of 9 kilometers every morning.
- She stops at a coffee shop after the walk, spending t minutes there.
- When walking at speed s km/h, the total time (walking + coffee shop) is 4 hours.
- When walking at speed s + 2 km/h, the total time is 2 hours 24 minutes (which is 2.4 hours), including the same t minutes at the coffee shop.
- The problem asks: If Aya walks at speed s + 0.5 km/h, what is the total time (walking + coffee shop) in minutes?

2. Analyze Relationships Between Components:
- The total time consists of walking time plus coffee shop time t.
- Walking time depends on speed and fixed distance: walking time = distance / speed.
- The coffee shop time t is constant across all scenarios.
- Two equations relate s, t, and total times:
  (9 / s) + (t / 60) = 4
  (9 / (s + 2)) + (t / 60) = 2.4
- These equations can be used to find s and t.
- Once s and t are known, the total time for speed s + 0.5 can be computed as (9 / (s + 0.5)) + (t / 60).

3. Identify the Field of Study:
- The problem is primarily algebraic, involving linear equations and rational expressions.
- It relates to applied mathematics, specifically rate-time-distance problems.
- Such problems are common in algebra courses and mathematical competitions.

4. Highlight Aspects Needing Clarification:
- The problem assumes constant speeds and a fixed coffee shop time t, which is reasonable.

- No ambiguity in the problem statement; all necessary information is provided.
- Assumptions: walking speed is constant during each scenario; coffee shop time t is the same in all cases.
