1. Extract and Summarize Given Information:
- Aya walks a fixed distance of 9 kilometers every morning.
- She stops at a coffee shop after the walk, spending t minutes there.
- When walking at speed s km/h, total time (walk + coffee) is 4 hours.
- When walking at speed s + 2 km/h, total time (walk + coffee) is 2 hours 24 minutes (which is 2.4 hours).
- The variable t represents the coffee shop time in minutes, which is constant in both scenarios.
- The problem asks about the total time (walk + coffee) when walking at speed s + 0.5 km/h.

2. Analyze Relationships Between Components:
- The total time includes walking time plus coffee time t.
- Walking time = distance / speed = 9 / speed hours.
- For speed s: 9/s + t/60 = 4 hours.
- For speed s + 2: 9/(s+2) + t/60 = 2.4 hours.
- These two equations link s and t.
- The coffee time t is constant and independent of walking speed.
- The problem involves interpolating or calculating total time for speed s + 0.5 km/h using these relationships.

- The problem is primarily in algebra and arithmetic.
- It involves solving linear equations with variables representing speed and time.
- Concepts include rates, time-distance relationships, and unit conversions.
