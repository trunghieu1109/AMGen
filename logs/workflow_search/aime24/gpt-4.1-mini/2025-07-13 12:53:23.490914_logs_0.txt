- When walking at speed s + 2 km/h, the total time (walking + coffee shop) is 2 hours 24 minutes (which is 2.4 hours).
- The variable t represents the coffee shop time in minutes, which is the same in both scenarios.
- The problem also considers walking at speed s + 0.5 km/h and asks for the total time including t.

2. Analyze Relationships Between Components:
- The total time includes walking time plus coffee shop time t.
- Walking time = distance / speed = 9 / speed hours.
- For speed s: 4 hours = (9 / s) + (t / 60).
- For speed s + 2: 2.4 hours = (9 / (s + 2)) + (t / 60).
- The coffee shop time t is constant and independent of walking speed.
- These two equations link s and t, allowing determination of both.
- The problem then asks for total time at speed s + 0.5, which would be (9 / (s + 0.5)) + (t / 60) hours.
